<p><ul><li>Primary Cell</li><li>Secondary Cell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dry cell battery market is divided into two main types: primary cells and secondary cells. Primary cells are non-rechargeable batteries, designed for single-use applications, commonly found in household devices like remote controls and flashlights. In contrast, secondary cells are rechargeable batteries, allowing multiple uses and recharges, making them ideal for applications such as smartphones and electric vehicles. The choice between these battery types depends on the intended use, longevity, and cost-efficiency for consumers and manufacturers alike.</p></p>
