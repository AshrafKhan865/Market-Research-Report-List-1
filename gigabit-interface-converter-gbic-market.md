<p><p>Gigabit Interface Converters (GBICs) are modular transceivers used to convert electrical signals into optical signals and vice versa in data communication networks. The market is categorized into single-mode and multimode GBICs. Single-mode GBICs transmit data over long distances using a single light wavelength, making them ideal for wide-area network applications. In contrast, multimode GBICs use multiple light paths for shorter distances, suitable for local area networks. Each type serves specific networking needs, influencing their demand and applications in the industry.</p></p>
