<p><p>The Low-E insulated glass market comprises hard-coated and soft-coated types, each offering distinct energy efficiency benefits. Hard-coated Low-E glass has a durable, metallic coating that reflects infrared energy while maintaining visible light transmission, making it suitable for commercial applications. In contrast, soft-coated Low-E glass features a more delicate coating that provides superior thermal performance and spectral selectivity, enhancing energy conservation in residential settings. Both types help reduce energy costs and improve indoor comfort by minimizing heat loss and solar gain.</p></p>
