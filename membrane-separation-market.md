<p><p>The membrane separation market encompasses various technologies used to separate and purify fluids. Reverse osmosis (RO) efficiently removes dissolved solids, while ultrafiltration (UF) targets larger particles and colloids. Microfiltration (MF) is used for suspensions and particulates, and nanofiltration (NF) bridges the gap between UF and RO, focusing on divalent ions and larger organic molecules. Other technologies may include pervaporation and electrodialysis, each tailored for specific applications in water treatment, food processing, and chemical production, driving market growth.</p></p>
