<p><p>The Short Glass Fiber Reinforced Polyamide market is primarily segmented into two molding types: injection molding and extrusion molding. Injection molding involves forcing molten polyamide mixed with short glass fibers into a mold, allowing for intricate shapes and high precision in manufacturing components. Extrusion molding, on the other hand, involves continuously forcing the fiber-reinforced material through a die to produce long shapes like sheets or rods. Both methods enhance the mechanical properties of polyamide, making it suitable for various industrial applications.</p></p>
