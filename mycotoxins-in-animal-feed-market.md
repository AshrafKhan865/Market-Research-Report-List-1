<p><p>The mycotoxins in animal feed market can be classified into two main types: Type I and Type II. Type I mycotoxins, such as aflatoxins and ochratoxins, are primarily produced by fungi like Aspergillus and Penicillium, and are known for their acute toxicity and impact on animal health and productivity. Type II mycotoxins include toxins like trichothecenes and zearalenone, which tend to cause chronic health issues and reproductive problems. Understanding these types is crucial for developing effective prevention and management strategies.</p></p>
