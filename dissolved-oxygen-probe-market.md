<p><p>The dissolved oxygen probe market includes primarily Galvanic and Polarographic probes, along with other specialized types. Galvanic probes rely on a chemical reaction that generates a small electrical current proportional to the oxygen concentration, offering continuous monitoring without the need for an external power source. Polarographic probes use an applied voltage to facilitate oxygen diffusion, requiring a power source and offering fast response times. Other probes may include optical sensors and resin-based technologies, catering to varied applications across industries such as water treatment and aquaculture.</p></p>
