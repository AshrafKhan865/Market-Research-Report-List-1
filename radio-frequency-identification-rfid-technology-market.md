<p><p>Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) technology operates across three primary market types: Low Frequency (LF), High Frequency (HF), and Ultra-High Frequency (UHF). LF operates between 30 kHz and 300 kHz, ideal for tracking animal tags and access control due to its short range and sensitivity. HF ranges from 3.3 MHz to 30 MHz, commonly used in contactless payments and library systems, while UHF, spanning 300 MHz to 3 GHz, enables long-range applications like inventory management and supply chain logistics. Each type serves distinct applications based on read distance and data transfer rates.</p></p>
