<p><p>The Native Cassava Starch market includes various types, primarily focusing on amylose and pullulan. Amylose is a linear starch component known for its gelling and thickening properties, widely used in food, pharmaceuticals, and biodegradable plastics. Pullulan, a polysaccharide produced by fermentation, is renowned for its film-forming ability and is commonly used in food coatings, edible films, and supplements. Both types cater to diverse applications due to their unique functional characteristics, driving market demand.</p></p>
