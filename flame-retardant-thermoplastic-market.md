<p><p>Flame-retardant thermoplastics are categorized into two main types: amorphous polymers and crystalline polymers. Amorphous polymers lack a defined structure, offering flexibility and impact resistance, making them suitable for applications requiring complex shapes. Examples include polycarbonate and polystyrene. Crystalline polymers, on the other hand, have a more ordered molecular structure, providing superior thermal stability and chemical resistance, often used in applications demanding durability. Both types are enhanced with flame-retardant additives to reduce flammability and improve safety in various industries.</p></p>
