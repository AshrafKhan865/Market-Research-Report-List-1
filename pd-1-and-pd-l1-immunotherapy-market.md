<p><p>PD-1 (Programmed Death-1) and PD-L1 (Programmed Death-Ligand 1) immunotherapy are innovative cancer treatments that leverage the body's immune system to fight tumors. PD-1 is a checkpoint protein on T cells that, when engaged with PD-L1 on tumor cells, inhibits the immune response, allowing cancer cells to evade detection. By blocking this interaction, PD-1 and PD-L1 inhibitors can reactivate T cells, enhancing the immune response against cancer and improving patient outcomes in various malignancies, including melanoma, lung cancer, and more.</p><p>The PD-1 and PD-L1 immunotherapy market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of cancer, advancements in biologics, and the rising adoption of personalized medicine.
