<p><p>Mouse ELISA kits are categorized based on the detection method and labeling strategies. Unconjugated kits utilize direct antigen detection with primary antibodies, providing a straightforward approach. Alkaline phosphatase kits use enzyme-linked antibodies that catalyze a color change, enhancing sensitivity and quantification. Biotin-labeled kits incorporate biotin-streptavidin systems to amplify signals, offering high specificity. Other types may include fluorescent or chemiluminescent methods, each catering to diverse experimental needs and offering unique advantages in research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
