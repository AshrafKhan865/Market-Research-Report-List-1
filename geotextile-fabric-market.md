<p><p>Geotextile fabrics are permeable materials used in construction and environmental projects for soil stabilization, drainage, and erosion control. The market is divided into **synthetic** and **natural** types. **Synthetic geotextiles**, made from polymers like polypropylene or polyester, are durable, resistant to chemicals, and widely used in heavy-duty applications. **Natural geotextiles**, crafted from materials like jute or coir, are biodegradable, eco-friendly, and ideal for temporary erosion control or vegetation support, often in environmentally sensitive areas. Both types cater to specific project needs and sustainability goals.</p></p>
