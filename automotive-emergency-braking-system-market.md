<p><p>The automotive emergency braking system market is categorized into two main types: foot brakes and hand brakes. Foot brakes, typically operated by the driver pressing a pedal, are the primary means of stopping a vehicle quickly during emergencies. Hand brakes, also known as parking brakes, are usually activated by pulling a lever or pressing a button, providing additional stopping power or securing the vehicle when parked. Both systems play critical roles in enhancing vehicle safety and preventing accidents.</p></p>
