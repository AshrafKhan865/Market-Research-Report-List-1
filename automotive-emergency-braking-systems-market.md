<p><p>The Automotive Emergency Braking Systems market encompasses two primary types: foot brakes and hand brakes. Foot brakes are the most common, activated by pressing the brake pedal, allowing for rapid deceleration during emergencies. They are typically integrated with advanced technologies like anti-lock braking systems (ABS) for enhanced safety. Hand brakes, usually lever-operated, provide additional stopping power and are often used as a secondary measure. Both systems are crucial for preventing accidents and ensuring vehicle control in critical situations.</p></p>
