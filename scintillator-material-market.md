<p><p>The scintillator material market is classified into three main types: solid scintillators, liquid scintillators, and gaseous scintillators. Solid scintillators, often made from inorganic crystals or plastics, are durable and widely used in radiation detection. Liquid scintillators utilize organic solvents mixed with fluorescent compounds, ideal for applications requiring high sensitivity. Gaseous scintillators operate in gas form, providing advantages in particle tracking and radiation measurement. Each type serves distinct applications in medical imaging, nuclear physics, and safety monitoring.</p></p>
