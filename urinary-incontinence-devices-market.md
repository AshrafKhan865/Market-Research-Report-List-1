<p><p>The Urinary Incontinence Devices market is divided into two main types: external and internal devices. External urinary incontinence devices, such as pads and external catheters, are designed to be worn outside the body, providing convenience and discretion for users. In contrast, internal urinary incontinence devices, including urinary catheters and artificial sphincters, are inserted into the body to manage urine flow directly. Both categories aim to enhance quality of life for individuals experiencing urinary incontinence by offering effective management solutions.</p></p>
